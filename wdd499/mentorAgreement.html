The mentor agreement form link will be sent to each student for completion at the beginning of the course.

This form is basically a signed agreement between you, your project mentor, and the instructor. Basic information will be requested and the mentor will be emailed separately, by the instructor, to confirm the agreement.

Mentor Agreement Description

I understand I must complete the Capstone Project Proposal to the satisfaction of my mentor. With his/her assistance, I will outline a project worthy of a working professional. My mentor must be a working professional in the web design or development or highly related field or the mentor can be a faculty member or instructor at BYU-Idaho.  Other mentor selections will need to be approved by the instructor. He/she will, in conjunction with my instructor, grade the quality and content of my capstone project and will need to be aware of aspects of the project throughout the process.

I understand that after my proposal is accepted I am required to meet with my mentor at least three times during the semester to track my progress. We will discuss my work on the project and I will bring samples, drafts, and plans for review and to receive feedback and constructive criticism.  I must meet with my instructor if my project is changed from my originally approved proposal.

I may be asked to present my project in some online venue to be determined, such as the CSE student works conference at the end of the term.

Filling out the form means that you agree to the terms and conditions of WDD 499.

The mentor will then need to agree, via email inquiry by the instructor to work with the student in generating and revising a worthy, professional-level proposal leading to a original, produced portfolio piece for the student.

Submission

To submit this assignment, just provide the date that you submitted the form. The form link will be sent to you or you can request it again from the instructor.
