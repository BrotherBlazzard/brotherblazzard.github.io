Description

Senior students will work closely with a professional mentor, faculty advisor, and/or peers to develop a project that demonstrates the fusion of their undergraduate coursework and experiences. This project should allow them to showcase what they have learned, as well as learn more about a topic that they would like to master in the industry, but did not have the opportunity to do so during their core and selected coursework in the degree program.

Course Outcomes

The purpose of the WDD Senior Capstone is to give students an opportunity to choose a significant project to complete that will allow them to showcase what they have learned, as well as choose a topic that they would like to master but did not have the opportunity to do so during their coursework.

Through their capstone experience, students will:

      • Apply what they have learned in the classroom to the creation of a significant web design/development project of their choosing.
      • Learn new skills.
      • Produce a professional quality project that could be used as a portfolio piece.

Course Content: Project

Each student will be required to propose a course project or work-based learning experience where direct reporting can be accounted for and documented. The project is open ended and student driven. This means each student should select a project that allows them to develop their skill set in an area of their own choosing ideally preparing them for the work force. The goal is to add tools to your working resume tool belt which includes a portfolio piece.

A final letter grade will be based upon the project selected and associated reporting and evaluation.

Project Proposal

Getting Started: Your first order of business is to create a proposal for your capstone project, paper or campaign. A proposal is like a blueprint and provides shape and direction. The more detail you include in the proposal the better your finished product. While the project type and content will vary between students, there are things everyone must address in an effective proposal. Below you will find the basic areas you need to cover.

Title: An effective title should help people clearly understand the project, its scope and your approach.

Summary of your project: What are you doing? How will you do it? If you cannot describe this in a tight, well-written paragraph or two you need to rethink the scope of your project. If you can describe it in just a few words, you may need to expand your vision. If it takes you multiple pages to describe your project it is too involved.

Justification and Professional Goals: The capstone is not about creating more student work; it is about learning something new and creating professional, quality content. You need to justify why your project is worthy of your 50 hours and how it will meet your professional and academic goals. Recycling an assignment from another class is not allowed. This should be uniquely tied to your proposed WDD degree. What do you want to do after graduation, and how will this capstone help you achieve this goal? You need to show how you are pushing your content knowledge and developing new skills.

Timetable: You will need to track all of the time you spend for this class. A projected timeline for your project execution, production and completion also is important.

Mentor

You are required to choose a mentor to work with for your project. This can be a professional, co-worker, or a faculty member or instructor in a web design/development related field.  It is preferred that you work with a professional. Your mentor will, in conjunction with your instructor, grade the quality and content of your capstone project and will need to be aware of aspects of the project throughout the process. Your mentor will approve and sign off on your project proposal before it goes to the instructor.

You should turn in your project proposal and signed mentor agreement within the first few weeks of the semester. You will be able to submit your project proposal online.

After your proposal is accepted you are required to meet with your mentor at least three times during the semester to track your progress. You will discuss your work on the project and you should bring samples, drafts, and plans for review and to receive feedback and constructive criticism. 

When choosing a mentor look for someone who has the knowledge and skills to help your accomplish your goals. They should be able to help you if you get stuck, and be willing and able to give you good detailed feedback to help you do your best work. Take advantage of your mentor! Communicate with them regularly, but make sure to respect their time. Do your part to make sure you are prepared for each meeting.

Report

Upon completion, but before presentation you should prepare a report detailing what you have learned by completing your project, and an accounting of time spent. (This usually takes 1-2 pages)

Presentation

At the completion of your project you will need to present it to the instructor and/or will be asked to make a digital report version that extends the information for placement in a to be determined location given the remote status of the campus in Spring 2020.
